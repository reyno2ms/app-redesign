Zara App Redesign

Introduction

Zara is widely used all around the globe to shop for trending clothes and accessories. Being able to navigate this clothing app is crucial for the customer to have an enjoyable app experience as well as easily find what they are looking for. Specifically for the Zara app, the navigation and hierarchy needs lots of improvement.

Design Question

How might we improve the navigation and hierarchy for users when shopping on the Zara app?

Defining Users and Goals

The main users of this app are teens and young adults who are looking for stylish options to add to their wardrobe. I decided to mainly focus of young adults aged 19-21 due to availability of resources around me.

Some of the goals most users have are:

  • Purchasing clothes.
  • Getting inspiration for fashion/outfits.
  • Easily able to search for items they have in mind.
  • Enjoy the experience of using the app enough that they will want to continue to use it.

The app before redesigning.

Zara has great clothing options and is used among lots of college students who want to revamp and upgrade their wardrobes, but it is very hard to navigate due to the fact that it is unorganized. For example, the menu button only gives some of the clothing options and to find the rest you have to go to the search tab at the bottom. There aren’t specific categories of clothes which is confusing to the user and looks scattered. This can cause a user to become uninterested in the app resulting in them closing out and finding elsewhere to shop.

Pain Points

As you can see in the images provided above, the home page doesn’t show much other than a few icons and the menu displayed at the bottom. When you click on the menu button a list of options pops up that just seems unorganized since it offers some of the clothing categories but then also offers “join life” and “Zaraseries” which is the about page and the inspiration page. This could come across as misleading or confusing to a user because if you click on the search icon at the bottom all the other clothing categories pop up. This could easily be fixed by condensing all the clothing items to one area and having the menu tab just be information or inspiration for their products.

User Research

The participants in this study were 13 college students who have been asked to complete this survey to help with project research.

Out of the 14 students, 71.4% (10 participants) have used the Zara app, and 21.4% (4 participants) have not.

Additionally, 64.3% of participants claimed that they had trouble using the app, and the other 35.7% of participants either haven’t used the app before or they said there were some features that were easy to use but otherwise it was a confusing app to navigate.

When asked if poor navigation led users to become less interesting in using an app, nearly 100% of the participants said yes.

The Redesign

During my research I asked participants what the most difficult part of using the app was, and almost every response said something along the lines of navigation problems causing confusion. When asked how these issues could be fixed, participants said if everything was condensed to one navigation pop up it would make the entire app experience easier.

To solve this issue I decided to move all the clothing categories into one area.

Wireframe/Mockup

Once redesigned, the navigation tabs looked like this . The initial homepage as well as the account button and the ‘My cart’ buttons were left the same. The buttons within the navigation menu such as the ‘search’ button and the ‘menu’ button at the bottom were changed significantly making it much easier to find exactly what the user needs.

Conclusion

After redesigning this app I hope that it will be much more user friendly and easier for people to use/understand. I have only ever heard negative things about how this app works and I hope that with the changes that were made it makes people want to start to use it again because the products offered are very high quality and trendy but the navigation drew people away from using the app at all.
